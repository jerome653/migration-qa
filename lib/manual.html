1 Installing the app

A one-time setup. Everything the app needs is inside the installer — nothing else to download. After this, it keeps itself up to date on its own.

  1. If you already have an older "SGEN Site QA", uninstall it first: Start menu → Settings → Apps → SGEN Site QA → Uninstall. (Only needed the first time.)
  2. Double-click SGEN Site QA Setup.exe (in this folder).
  3. It installs and opens on its own — no options to fiddle with. That's it.
Windows SmartScreen? If Windows shows a blue "Windows protected your PC" box, click More info → Run anyway. This happens because the app isn't signed by a big vendor — it's safe; everything runs on your machine.

2 The main screen

Everything lives in one window. Here's what you're looking at.

  • The top bar stays put as you scroll. On the right you'll find Logs (version history + support), Manual (this guide), and ? Help (the walkthrough, any time).
  • The four tabs below it are the four tools. Click a tab to switch.
  • The big area is where you type a website address and see the results.
First time? A short guided tour pops up automatically and points at each part of the screen. You can reopen it any time from ? Help.

3 The four tools

Pick the one that matches what you want to know.

1 Site Audit

Checks one website for problems — broken links, forms, mobile layout, accessibility, speed, security, and more. Start here.

2 Visual Comparison

Puts two websites (an old one and a new one) side by side and highlights what looks different, at every screen size.

3 Post-Deployment Check

After moving a site to a new build, confirms every page, image, menu and form actually made it across.

4 Reports

Every check you've run, kept and grouped by website. Reopen one, or save it as a PDF to share.

4 Running your first check

Let's do a Site Audit — the most common one.

  1. Make sure you're on the Site Audit tab.
  2. Type a website address in Site URL — for example sgen.com. You don't need to type "https://".
  3. Leave the settings as they are (they're already sensible), then click the red Run audit button.
  4. Wait a few seconds while it works. When it's done, the full report appears right below.
Your settings are remembered. Anything you change here — the address, the options, which screen sizes to check — is saved automatically, so you don't set it up again next time.

The other tools work the same way: fill in the box(es) at the top, then click the red button. If a check is taking too long you can Cancel it, and Retry if it fails.

5 Reading your results

The report is built to answer one question first: is this site OK or not?

Start at the top — the bottom line

The big circle shows the share of checks that passed. Next to it, an Overall result tells you plainly whether the site is ready or needs attention, and how many checks are failing. This is the pass/fail answer.

The numbers below are quality scores, not pass/fail

Under the top result you'll see scores from 0 to 100 (by dimension and by section). These are quality reads to help you dig in — they are not separate verdicts. So a "100" in one place and a "58" in another aren't contradicting each other; they're measuring different things.

What the colours mean

Red = passing / good Grey = warning Silver = failed Dark = needs a human to check
Tip: In this app, red is good — it's the SGEN brand colour for "passing". The lighter and greyer a score is, the more attention it needs.

Jump straight to problems

The summary cards near the top (Passed, Warnings, Failed, and so on) are clickable — click Failed and the report scrolls to exactly those items. Every issue has a Copy for dev button that copies a ready-to-paste ticket for whoever fixes it.

You can also Save as PDF to hand the whole report to someone else.

6 Finding past reports

Every check is saved automatically. The Reports tab keeps them tidy.

  • Reports are grouped by website, newest first.
  • Use the search box to find a site by name, and the date filter (Any time / Today / Last 7 days / Last 30 days) to narrow things down.
  • Click any run to preview it, open the full HTML, or Save as PDF.

Everything stays on your computer — nothing is uploaded anywhere.

7 Keeping it up to date

Updates are tiny and take about a second. You're always in control.

  • The app quietly checks for updates on its own. When one is ready, the Check for updates button turns red and a small message pops up.
  • Click the red button to download it (a small file — about a second).
  • Then click Restart to update. The app blinks once and comes back on the new version.
No big reinstalls. After the first install, you never run an installer again — updates happen right inside the app.

8 If something looks off

There's a one-click way to send us exactly what's happening.

  1. Click Logs in the top-right.
  2. Click Generate diagnostics.
  3. Click Download .md (or Copy to clipboard) and send that file to your contact for review.

The diagnostics file lists your version, your computer's setup, your update history, recent runs, and the app's recent activity log — enough to pinpoint almost anything. It contains no personal data beyond that.

9 Quick answers

Does the installer include everything, or does it download more?
Everything is inside the installer — the app, its browser engine, and all its libraries. Once it's installed you can run checks straight away, even offline. The only time it uses the internet is to reach the website you're checking, and to see if a newer version of the app is available.
Does this send my data anywhere?
No. All checks run on your own computer. The only thing the app contacts the internet for is (a) the website you ask it to check, and (b) checking whether a new version of the app is available.
Why is a passing score shown in red?
Red is SGEN's brand colour, and here it means "good / passing". Greyer, lighter colours mean warnings or failures. There's a colour key on every report.
The address box says the site can't be reached.
Double-check the address is spelled correctly and the site is online. You don't need to type "https://" — just the domain, like example.com.
A check is taking a long time.
Larger sites take longer, especially with "Browser render" on. You can click Cancel at any time, or lower Max pages to 1 for a quick single-page check.
Do I need to install updates manually?
No. The app tells you when one is ready (the button turns red). You just click it, then Restart. No installer, no admin rights.
Where are my reports saved?
On your computer, inside the app's own data folder. Use the Reports tab to find them, or save any of them as a PDF to keep or share.
